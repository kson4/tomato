WHY GROW YOUR OWN TOMATOES?

Many people do not like tomatoes. However, I would argue that many people have not had the pleasure of trying a "real" tomato. You see, a tomato that is fully ripe would be an absolute nightmare to ship out to your local grocery stores. The slightest touch would easily leave an indent and ruin its marketability. A close analogy I can give of this would be similar to squeezing a ripe kiwi, or even an avocado.

Therefore, farmers would purposefully harvest their tomatoes while they are still green, giving them more durability. Like many fruits, tomatoes will still ripen off the vine. By the time the tomatoes reach your local grocery store, they will have ripen just enough to change color, but hardly any further.

However, tomatoes that were not given the adequate chance to fully ripen, are significantly inferior. They lack the sweetness and flavor that many homegrown tomatoes have.

STILL NOT CONVINCED?

HEAD OVER TO YOUR LOCAL FARMER'S MARKET

Once you get there, keep a look out for heirloom tomatoes. If you do not have one nearby, my local Sprouts, Trader Joes, Amazon Fresh, and Whole Foods have them as well. However, I would still encourage to try them out. I do not mean to exaggerate but, my life was completely changed afterwards. They are now my new found obsession. However, you may have noticed that they are still too expensive
